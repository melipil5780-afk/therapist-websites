Struggling with Depression?

Discover the Hidden Causes Behind Your Emotional Pain

Meli Pil, MHC-LP
Mental Health Counselor, Limited Permit
IN PERSON SESSIONS ONLY

Many people ask what causes depression or deep sadness. Depression is a symptom experienced by many, stemming from a variety of underlying issues. Similar to how a fever signals a physical problem in the body, depression indicates that something is going wrong in our emotional world.

While the specific cause of depression varies from person to person, there are common factors that help us understand the root of this pain:

1. Learned Helplessness (Martin Seligman)

This occurs when individuals repeatedly experience uncontrollable negative events. They "learn" that they have no control over their circumstances, leading them to stop trying to change even when opportunities arise. This state of learned helplessness is a significant driver of depressive symptoms.

2. Self-Treatment Mechanisms from Childhood

As children, we develop coping strategies—like dissociation or shutting down—to survive difficult environments. In adulthood, these outdated mechanisms can become traps that manifest as depression.

3. Dissociation & Numbness

When emotions become too overwhelming to process, the mind may disconnect. This results in profound numbness or feeling "dead inside," which is a core component of many depressive experiences.

4. Avoidance of Painful Emotions

Avoiding specific feelings like grief or shame leads to a buildup of unprocessed emotions. These suppressed feelings eventually weigh the person down, manifesting as chronic depression.

5. Lack of Purpose or Meaning

Following Viktor Frankl’s logotherapy, a lack of personal meaning can lead to an "existential void," where deep sadness takes root because life feels directionless.

6. Decision Paralysis

When overwhelmed by conflicting values or impossible choices, an individual can enter a "freeze" state. This paralysis drains energy and leads to a state of depressive collapse.

If you are feeling these symptoms, reaching out is the first step toward reclaiming your agency.
