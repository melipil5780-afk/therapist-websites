Depression is more than a low mood; it is often a complex, multi-layered response to unmet needs, biological factors, and learned behaviors. Understanding the "Why" is the first step toward the "How."

The 7 Core Factors

1. Biology: Genetics and brain chemistry (neurotransmitters like serotonin) play a foundational role in how we experience joy and stability.
2. Psychology: Learned helplessness—the belief that we have no control over our pain—often keeps us trapped in a cycle of despair.
3. Social/Relational: Isolation or toxic relationship dynamics can reinforce a sense of worthlessness.
4. Internalized Blueprints: Maladaptive patterns formed in childhood that tell us we are defective or destined to fail.
5. Emotional Suppression: When we are unable to express "forbidden" emotions (like anger), they often turn inward, manifesting as depression.
6. Trauma & Loss: Unprocessed grief or significant life stressors can overwhelm the nervous system.
7. Purpose & Identity: A disconnect between your daily life and your authentic self often creates a profound sense of emptiness.

In our work, we look at the intersection of these factors to build a personalized path toward recovery.
