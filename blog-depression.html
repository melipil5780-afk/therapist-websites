Many people ask what causes depression or deep sadness. Depression is a symptom experienced by many, stemming from a variety of underlying issues. Similar to how a fever signals a physical problem in the body, depression indicates that something is going wrong in our emotional world.

Common Factors in Emotional Pain

While the specific cause of depression varies from person to person, there are common factors that help us understand the root of this pain:

1. Learned Helplessness: Coined by Martin Seligman, this occurs when individuals repeatedly experience uncontrollable negative events. They "learn" that they have no control, eventually leading to a state of collapse and despair.

2. Self-Treatment Mechanisms: Many develop coping mechanisms in childhood, like dissociation, to survive overwhelming environments. These "life-savers" often become maladaptive in adulthood, contributing to the cycle of depression.

3. Dissociation: When the mind decides it is "too much to feel," it shuts the lights off. This leads to profound feelings of emptiness or numbness, where the individual feels "dead inside."

4. Avoidance & Suppression: Avoiding painful emotions leads to a buildup of unprocessed feelings. Suppressed emotions manifest as the heavy, weighted feeling we recognize as depression.

6. Decision Paralysis: Being overwhelmed by impossible choices or conflicting internal values can cause a "freeze" state, draining the individual of all vital energy.
