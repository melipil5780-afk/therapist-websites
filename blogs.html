Struggling with Depression?

Discover the Hidden Causes Behind Your Emotional Pain

By Meli Pil, MHC-LP | December 13, 2024

Many people ask what causes depression or deep sadness. Depression is a symptom experienced by many, stemming from a variety of underlying issues. Similar to how a fever signals a physical problem in the body, depression indicates that something is going wrong in our emotional world. Here are the hidden causes:

1. Learned Helplessness

Coined by Martin Seligman, this occurs when individuals repeatedly experience uncontrollable negative events. They "learn" that they have no control over their circumstances. Even when opportunities for change arise, they may feel paralyzed, believing that no effort will make a difference.

2. Self-Treatment Mechanisms from Childhood

As children, we develop coping strategies (like shutting down or worrying) to survive difficult environments. In adulthood, these outdated mechanisms can become traps. For example, constant rumination—trying to "think" your way out of feelings—can actually deepen depression.

3. Dissociation & Numbness

When emotions are too overwhelming to feel, the mind may disconnect. This is called dissociation. While it protects you from pain in the moment, it leads to a sense of emptiness, numbness, or feeling "dead inside"—a core component of severe depression.

4. Avoidance of Painful Emotions

We often unconsciously avoid specific painful feelings (like grief or shame). Instead of feeling them, we might experience secondary emotions—like vague frustration or irritability—while the primary emotion remains stuck and unprocessed.

5. Confusion & Decision Paralysis

When we are overwhelmed by conflicting values or choices, we can become paralyzed. The inability to move forward or make decisions drains our energy and can lead to a state of depressive collapse.
