6 Principles for Working With Emotions

In our therapy work, we don't just talk about problems; we engage with the emotional energy behind them. Use these six principles as your roadmap for emotional transformation.

01

Awareness

The first step is moving from "being" the emotion to observing it. Awareness allows you to feel the physical sensation of an emotion in your body without being consumed by it. We work to identify where the emotion lives—perhaps a tightness in the chest or a knot in the stomach—creating the space necessary for processing.

02

Expression

True healing requires moving beyond secondary emotions (like anger or frustration) to find the primary emotion beneath (like loneliness, sadness, or shame). Authentic expression is about naming the unacknowledged and allowing the energy of that emotion to be seen and symbolized.

03

Regulation

Emotions can sometimes feel like a tidal wave. Regulation isn't about suppression; it's about learning to manage the intensity so you stay within your "window of tolerance." This ensures that the work of therapy remains safe and productive rather than overwhelming.

04

Reflection

Every emotion is a messenger. Reflection is the process of asking: "What is this feeling telling me about my needs, my boundaries, or my values?" We use this insight to make sense of your internal world and your relationships with others.

05

Transformation

This is where the shift happens. We use "healthy" emotional states—such as self-compassion, assertive anger, or protective grief—to transform stuck, maladaptive feelings. We replace the old "Inner Blueprints" with new emotional experiences that align with who you are today.

06

Corrective Emotional Experience

Healing often happens in the "here and now." By exploring the relational dynamics that surface during our sessions, we create a real-time experience of safety, connection, and empowerment. This experience acts as the ultimate antidote to the neglect or trauma of the past.
