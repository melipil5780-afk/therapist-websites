Listening to Emotion

Clinical Reflection

We often treat emotions as interruptions—unwanted noise that gets in the way of our productivity or our peace. We try to think our way out of them, or wait for them to simply pass.

But in depth-oriented therapy, we view emotions differently: as signals. An emotion is often the psyche's way of trying to bring our attention to a need that is going unmet or a boundary that has been crossed.

The 6 Principles of Emotional Awareness

A roadmap for when feelings begin to feel overwhelming:

  • Naming: Moving from "I feel bad" to "I feel dismissed."
  • Localization: Where is the feeling sitting in the body?
  • Validation: Acknowledging the feeling makes sense given your history.
  • Investigation: What is the "hidden" request this emotion is making?
  • Non-Judgment: Feelings are data, not moral failings.
  • Expression: Finding a safe container to let the feeling move.

When a feeling persists, it is usually because it hasn't been fully heard yet. By slowing down and staying close to the experience, the "noise" begins to transform into insight. This is where the work of change truly begins.

Understanding your emotional landscape isn't about control; it's about relationship. It’s about learning to trust your internal compass once again.
