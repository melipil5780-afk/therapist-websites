Therapy that works at the pace of the nervous system

Change does not arrive through force or insight alone. It unfolds when the mind and body are allowed to settle, sense, and respond in their own time.

Experience before explanation

Rather than beginning with interpretation, the work starts with what is already present—emotion, sensation, relational patterns—noticed gently, without urgency.

Where emotion lives

Feelings are not abstract concepts. They emerge in posture, breath, tone, and impulse. When the body is included, something shifts that talking alone cannot reach.

Integration, not intensity

The goal is not catharsis for its own sake, but integration—so what changes here can be carried into daily life with steadiness and clarity.
