The Guide

This work begins by slowing down. By attending closely to what is already present — thoughts forming, emotions shifting, sensations arising.

Together, we listen for patterns that quietly shape experience, often without conscious awareness, and bring them into a space where they can be understood rather than repeated.

The Live Encounter

Therapy here is not an abstract discussion about life, but a live encounter with how life is being experienced in the moment.

We pay attention to what happens between us — to shifts in feeling, hesitation, resonance, tension — allowing experience to become thinkable as it unfolds.

Over time, what once felt automatic begins to soften. New meanings emerge. Choice becomes possible.
