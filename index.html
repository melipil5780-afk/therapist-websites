STEP ONE

Arrival

Stand before the threshold. Feel your breath, your body, and the ground beneath you. This is where transformation begins‚Äîin the simple act of arriving fully present.

STEP TWO

Chair Work

Two chairs face each other. One holds you. The other holds a part of you or a loved one. Through dialogue, we bring what's internal into the space between us.

STEP THREE

Movement

Anger, grief, fear‚Äîthey live in the body. Strike cushions, push against walls, stomp, shake, roar. Let the body speak what words cannot.

STEP FOUR

Sensing

Pause. Feel what remains. Notice the heat, the tingling, the spaciousness. In this stillness, wisdom emerges from the depths of your experience.

STEP FIVE

Voice

Name what moved. Connect sensation to meaning and turn raw energy into insight. Your story finds its voice, not from the mind alone, but from the whole of you.

STEP SIX

Integration

Carry the shift into your world. This is not just a session; it is a new way of being. Integration means living the change, moment by moment.

Common Questions

Everything you need to know about beginning your therapeutic journey

What is experiential psychotherapy? +

Experiential psychotherapy is a therapeutic approach that emphasizes experiencing emotions and sensations in the present moment, rather than just talking about them. It includes techniques like chair work, body awareness, and movement to access and process deep emotional material that may be difficult to reach through conversation alone.

How is this different from traditional talk therapy? +

While traditional talk therapy focuses primarily on cognitive understanding, experiential therapy engages the whole person‚Äîmind, body, and emotions. We use active techniques to help you feel and process emotions in real-time, leading to deeper and often faster transformation.

What should I expect in my first session? +

Your first session will begin with us getting to know each other and understanding what brings you to therapy. I'll explain my approach and we'll discuss your goals. We may begin some gentle experiential work if you're comfortable, but there's no pressure‚Äîwe'll move at your pace.

Do you accept insurance? +

I am currently an out-of-network provider. I can provide you with a superbill (detailed receipt) that you can submit to your insurance company for potential reimbursement, depending on your out-of-network benefits.

How long does therapy typically last? +

The duration varies based on individual needs and goals. Some clients find significant shifts within 8-12 sessions, while others prefer ongoing support. We'll regularly check in about your progress and adjust our work together as needed.
