Listening to Emotion

Lived Experience

An emotion is often the psyche's way of trying to bring our attention to a need that is going unmet or a boundary that has been crossed. We treat them as noise, but they are actually data.

Clinical Grounding

In our work, we use a framework to translate these signals:

By naming the specific vibration of a feeling—moving from "upset" to "lonely" or "invalidated"—we begin to unlock the choice of what to do next.

When a feeling persists, it is usually because it hasn't been fully heard yet. By slowing down and staying close to the experience, the "noise" begins to transform into insight.
